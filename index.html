Program

The 2020 summer Olympic and Paralympic Games and the Rugby World Cup (RWC) are the largest and third largest sporting events in the world (the FIFA World Cup being the second largest). The staging of these events provides an unparalleled opportunity for the host country and city – essentially a global “commercial”. The years leading up to these events can also be used to generate significant long-term business outcomes. As the host country for both the RWC in 2019 and the Olympic Games in 2020, Japan has a unique opportunity to not only present itself to the world but to also shape future perceptions of its 21st century economic and business credentials.

Purpose

The primary objective of Japan Access 2020 will be to help RWC and Olympic Games’ sponsors maximise their financial investment in these events. It will do so by assisting these companies with their international business expansion, market development and partnership programs.

New Trade

Identifying and enabling new trade and investment opportunities.

International Partnership

Designing and facilitating new and uniquely focused international business partnering opportunities

Global Business

Developing and supporting the implementation of international business development marketing initiatives.

Background

Investment 2000 and China Access 2008 - the official business legacy programs of the Sydney 2000 and Beijing 2008 Olympic Games - were the first truly international programs to use the Olympic Games in this way. Both generated significant economic benefits for their respective host cities and countries, and tangible business outcomes for their sponsoring (partner) companies. (An overview of these initiatives will be attached as clickable documents.) Japan Access 2020 intends to build on the successes, learnings and contacts generated by these programs to produce similar outcomes for Japan. It will also leverage the international experience and contacts of its principals, all of whom were involved in the Sydney 2000 and Beijing 2008 initiatives.

Activities

1

Working individually with JA2020 partner companies to design and implement a tailored strategy to meet their international business goals

2

A systematic program of focused business events both inside and outside Japan. These events will provide exposure for the partner companies as well as reaching out to an international audience of potential partners, suppliers, new customers and investment opportunities. In the period 2017-2020 it is envisaged that this program would entail a minimum of twelve high quality business events in Europe, North America and Australasia, and at least two annual events within Japan.

Structure

Both Investment 2000 and China Access were developed and led by the private sector. This unique feature was unequivocally the fundamental factor in their success. Both initiatives had the full support and involvement of government and relevant sporting organizations, but they were business-to-business initiatives. This operating model ensures that the focus is always on creating real business outcomes for all participating parties. Similarly Japan Access 2020 will be a private sector activity with a consortium of leading Japanese RWC 2020 and/or Tokyo 2020 sponsors as partners. These partner organizations will be publicly recognized as taking a corporate leadership role in helping to ensure that Japan maximizes the economic, business and social benefits that can arise from the staging of the 2019 RWC and 2020 Olympic Games.
